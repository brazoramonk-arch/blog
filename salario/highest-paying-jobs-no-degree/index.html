Highest Paying Jobs Without a Degree in 2026

A four-year degree is no longer the only path to a high-paying career. From skilled trades to tech to aviation, dozens of careers offer six-figure earning potential without college. This guide covers the 20 best-paying options, what they actually require, and how to get started.

March 9, 2026 15 min read

The Degree Requirement Is Disappearing

A fundamental shift is happening in the American labor market. In 2026, over 60% of U.S. adults do not hold a bachelor's degree, yet the economy desperately needs skilled workers across dozens of high-paying fields. Major employers have responded by dropping degree requirements: Google, Apple, IBM, Delta Airlines, Walmart, Bank of America, and the entire U.S. federal government have all removed degree requirements from most job postings in recent years.

The reasons are practical. A labor shortage of over 650,000 workers in the skilled trades alone is driving wages up dramatically. Meanwhile, the average cost of a four-year degree has crossed $160,000 at private universities, and student loan debt exceeds $1.77 trillion nationally. Employers are realizing that skills, certifications, and experience predict job performance better than a diploma.

This is not about settling for less. Many of the jobs on this list pay $80,000 to $200,000+ per year while requiring zero college debt. Use our Salary Calculator to see what any of these salaries look like after taxes in your state.

Top 10 Highest Paying Jobs Without a Degree

1. Air Traffic Controller

Median Salary: $137,380 | Top 10%: $192,000+

Air traffic controllers guide aircraft through takeoff, landing, and flight paths. The FAA requires candidates to be U.S. citizens, pass medical and security clearances, and complete the FAA Academy. No degree is required, though you must apply through USAJobs before age 31. The job is high-stress but comes with federal benefits including a pension, and controllers can retire as early as age 50 with 20 years of service. Use our salary to hourly converter to see what this breaks down to per hour.

2. Software Developer (Self-Taught/Bootcamp)

Median Salary: $120,000 | Top 10%: $200,000+

Tech is the most accessible high-paying field for non-degree holders. Companies like Google, Apple, and Meta have dropped degree requirements for many engineering roles. Coding bootcamps (12-16 weeks, $10,000-$20,000) or self-study through free resources can prepare you for entry-level roles. The investment pays back within months at these salary levels. Junior developers typically start at $70,000-$90,000 and reach six figures within 2-3 years.

3. Commercial Pilot

Median Salary: $103,910 | Top 10%: $208,000+

Regional airlines are so desperate for pilots that many have dropped degree requirements entirely. You need a commercial pilot license (CPL) and instrument rating, which requires approximately 250 flight hours and costs $50,000-$80,000 at flight schools. Some regional airlines now offer tuition reimbursement programs. Senior captains at major airlines earn $300,000+, though reaching a major airline typically takes 5-10 years at a regional carrier.

4. Elevator Installer and Repairer

Median Salary: $102,420 | Top 10%: $131,000+

One of the highest-paying trade jobs in America, elevator work requires a 4-year apprenticeship but no degree. The work involves installing, maintaining, and repairing elevators, escalators, and moving walkways. The field has strong union representation (IUEC), which keeps wages high and provides excellent benefits. Job growth is projected at 6% through 2032, driven by new building construction and aging elevator infrastructure.

5. Power Plant Operator

Median Salary: $94,790 | Top 10%: $120,000+

Power plant operators control the systems that generate electricity. Most receive on-the-job training and need to pass certification exams from the Nuclear Regulatory Commission (for nuclear plants) or plant-specific licensing. The energy transition is creating new demand for operators at solar, wind, and battery storage facilities. Check what this salary means in your state with our Net Pay Calculator.

6. Tech Sales (SaaS Account Executive)

Base Salary: $70,000-$90,000 | OTE (On-Target Earnings): $140,000-$250,000+

SaaS sales is one of the fastest paths to a six-figure income without a degree. Companies like Salesforce, HubSpot, and Oracle hire based on communication skills and drive, not credentials. Most start as a Sales Development Rep (SDR) at $50,000-$70,000 OTE, then promote to Account Executive within 12-18 months. Top AEs at enterprise companies regularly earn $200,000-$400,000 in total compensation. Learn how commission structures work to understand sales compensation.

7. Electrician

Median Salary: $61,590 | Master Electrician/Business Owner: $100,000-$200,000+

Electricians are in extreme demand due to EV charging infrastructure, solar installations, and data center construction. A 4-5 year apprenticeship leads to journeyman status, and master electricians who start their own shops commonly earn $150,000-$250,000. The work cannot be outsourced or automated, providing exceptional job security. For more on construction trades, visit HammerIO for construction estimating tools.

8. Plumber

Median Salary: $60,090 | Master Plumber/Owner: $100,000-$200,000+

Like electricians, plumbers benefit from a massive labor shortage. The average plumber is over 55, and not enough young workers are entering the trade. This demographic squeeze is driving wages up rapidly. After a 4-5 year apprenticeship, master plumbers in high-demand metro areas can charge $100-$200/hour. Business owners with a crew frequently earn $200,000+. Convert these hourly rates to annual salary with our Hourly to Salary Calculator.

9. HVAC Technician

Median Salary: $57,300 | Experienced/Specialized: $80,000-$120,000+

HVAC technicians install, repair, and maintain heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems. Training takes 6 months to 2 years through trade school or apprenticeship. Technicians who specialize in commercial systems or refrigeration earn significantly more. The heat pump transition and growing demand for energy-efficient buildings are creating new opportunities. Learn about energy industry careers at JouleIO.

10. Real Estate Agent

Median Income: $52,030 | Top Agents: $150,000-$500,000+

Real estate requires a license (60-180 hours of coursework depending on state) but no degree. Income is commission-based and varies wildly. Top-producing agents in expensive markets earn $200,000-$500,000+. The first 1-2 years are typically lean while you build your network. Understanding how commission works is essential before entering this field.

How to Get Started Without a Degree

Breaking into a high-paying career without a degree requires a strategic approach. Here is a practical roadmap:

For Tech Careers

  • Start learning immediately. Free resources like freeCodeCamp, The Odin Project, and CS50 on edX provide world-class education at zero cost.
  • Build a portfolio. Three to five real projects matter more than any credential. Build tools people actually use.
  • Get certified. AWS, Google Cloud, CompTIA, and Salesforce certifications signal competence to hiring managers.
  • Contribute to open source. GitHub activity is your resume. It shows you can work on real codebases with other developers.

For Skilled Trades

  • Find an apprenticeship. The Department of Labor's ApprenticeshipUSA program lists thousands of registered apprenticeships. You earn while you learn.
  • Contact your local union. IBEW (electricians), UA (plumbers/pipefitters), and IUEC (elevator installers) offer structured apprenticeship programs with excellent training and benefits.
  • Consider trade school. Programs take 6 months to 2 years and cost $5,000-$15,000, a fraction of a four-year degree.
  • Get licensed. Most states require trade licenses after completing an apprenticeship. Check your state's requirements early.

For Sales Careers

  • Start as an SDR. Sales Development Rep roles are the entry point. Companies hire based on energy and communication skills.
  • Learn the tools. Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, and LinkedIn Sales Navigator are the platforms you will use daily.
  • Read foundational books. "SPIN Selling," "The Challenger Sale," and "Never Split the Difference" are industry essentials.

Whatever path you choose, understand the full financial picture before making a decision. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the highest paying job without a degree?

Air traffic controllers earn a median of $137,380 with no degree required. In tech, self-taught software developers can earn $120,000+. Trade business owners (electricians, plumbers) commonly earn $150,000-$250,000. Tech sales Account Executives regularly exceed $150,000 in total compensation.

Are trade jobs still worth it in 2026?

Absolutely. A shortage of 650,000+ trade workers is driving wages up significantly. Electricians, plumbers, and HVAC techs earn $60,000-$100,000+ with experience, and business owners often earn $150,000-$250,000. Trades workers also avoid $50,000-$200,000 in student debt, giving them a multi-year head start on building wealth.

Can you become a software developer without a degree?

Yes. Google, Apple, IBM, and many other companies have dropped degree requirements. Bootcamp graduates and self-taught developers regularly earn $80,000-$150,000+. Focus on building a portfolio, contributing to open source, and earning certifications. Expect to invest 6-18 months of intensive learning before being job-ready.
