Software Engineer Salary: 2026 Guide by Level & Location

The BLS puts median software developer pay at $130,160 — but that number masks a staggering range from $68,000 to well over $500,000. Here is what the data actually shows, and what determines which end of that range you land on.

March 24, 2026 15 min read

Key Takeaways

  • BLS 2024 median for software developers: $130,160 — but this covers all company types and industries
  • California leads state pay at $170,910 median; Washington offers better after-tax value with zero state income tax
  • ML/AI specialists earn 20–40% more than general software engineers at the same experience level
  • Big tech total comp (base + bonus + equity) runs 2–3x higher than non-tech companies at equivalent levels
  • The single most impactful salary lever is not your skill set — it is which company you work for

The Myth of the "Average" Software Engineer Salary

Here is the number you will see cited everywhere: $130,160. That is the median annual wage for software developers per the Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS) survey, published in May 2024, covering 1.79 million professionals across the country.

The problem is that this number simultaneously describes:

  • A junior developer at a regional bank in Tennessee earning $78,000
  • A mid-level engineer at Stripe in San Francisco earning $340,000 total compensation
  • A principal engineer at a hedge fund in New York earning $900,000

The BLS figure is a useful labor market baseline, but it is meaningless without context. According to Glassdoor data from March 2026 — based on 712,834 salary submissions — the 25th percentile base salary is $118,785 and the 75th percentile is $189,039. That $70,000+ spread at just one standard deviation illustrates why the "average" figure misleads more than it informs.

What actually drives your software engineer salary is a combination of four factors: experience level, company tier, geographic location, and specialization. Let us break down each one with real data.

Software Engineer Salary by Experience Level

Experience level is the most consistent salary driver across all company types. Per Levels.fyi 2025 End of Year Report — aggregating compensation data from over 500,000 verified salary submissions — here is how pay scales with seniority across company tiers:

Experience Title Base (All) TC (Big Tech) TC (Non-Tech)
0–2 years Junior / New Grad $75K–$110K $190K–$230K $70K–$100K
2–5 years Mid-Level / SDE II $100K–$145K $250K–$350K $100K–$145K
5–9 years Senior Engineer $130K–$175K $350K–$500K $140K–$200K
9–14 years Staff Engineer $160K–$220K $500K–$750K $180K–$280K
14+ years Principal / Sr. Staff $180K–$260K $700K–$1.2M $250K–$400K

TC = Total Compensation (base + annual bonus + equity, annualized). "Big Tech" includes Google, Meta, Apple, Amazon, Microsoft, Netflix, Stripe. Source: Levels.fyi 2025 End of Year Compensation Report.

One data point stands out: the gap between big tech and non-tech is widest at the senior and staff levels. A 7-year engineer at a Fortune 500 non-tech company might earn $160,000 base. The same engineer at Google earns $400,000+ in total compensation. That $240,000 annual difference compounds dramatically over a career.

The Promotion Timeline Reality

According to Levels.fyi data, the average promotion timeline is 18–24 months between junior and mid-level, and 2–4 years from mid-level to senior. The jump from senior to staff is where careers often stall — it is a qualitative change in scope, not just tenure. Washington vs California — the real comparison: California's $170,910 median looks superior, but California's top marginal state income tax rate of 13.3% is the highest in the country. Texas is the jobs leader: Texas employs more software engineers in absolute terms than any other state, driven by Austin, Dallas, and Houston tech corridors. The median is lower than coastal states, but zero state income tax and significantly lower housing costs make Texas increasingly attractive.

The Specialization Premium: Which Skills Pay More

Not all software engineering roles are compensated equally. Specialization in high-demand areas can add 15–40% to your base salary compared to general software engineering roles. Here is the specialty premium landscape for 2026:

Machine Learning / AI Engineering

Designing, training, and deploying ML models at scale. Requires strong math background (linear algebra, statistics, probability).

+20–40%

Security Engineering

Vulnerability research, penetration testing, security architecture. Critical shortage drives premium pricing.

+15–25%

Platform / Infrastructure Engineering

Developer tooling, cloud infrastructure, internal platforms. Highly valued at companies operating at scale.

+10–20%

Quantitative / Trading Systems

Low-latency systems for hedge funds and prop trading firms. Highest ceiling of any engineering specialty.

+50–200%

Data Engineering

Building data pipelines, warehouses, and analytics infrastructure. Strong demand from every industry vertical.

+5–15%

The AI engineering premium has expanded significantly since 2023. Per Levels.fyi data, senior ML engineers at major tech companies earned $450,000–$700,000 total compensation in 2025 — a 25% increase over 2023 levels as demand for AI talent far outpaces supply.

What $130K Actually Takes Home

Understanding gross salary is only half the picture. A software engineer earning the BLS median of $130,160 takes home very different amounts depending on their state and deductions. Here are three scenarios at the same gross salary:

Texas $130,160 ~$22,800 $0 ~$97,400
New York $130,160 ~$22,800 ~$9,200 ~$88,200

Estimates assume single filer, standard deduction, 2026 tax brackets, FICA included. Actual take-home will vary based on deductions, 401(k) contributions, and other factors.

How to Know if Your Salary Is Competitive

Benchmarking your compensation requires more than a Google search. Here is a systematic approach used by experienced HR compensation analysts:

  1. Start with role-level, not title. "Senior Engineer" at a 50-person startup is not the same as "Senior Engineer" at Amazon. Research by company size, funding stage, and industry vertical, not just job title.
  2. Use multiple data sources. Cross-reference Glassdoor (large sample, self-reported), Levels.fyi (verified tech comp data), and BLS OEWS (comprehensive but lagged by 12–18 months). Each has different coverage and methodology.
  3. Include total compensation, not just base. A $150,000 base with strong equity may beat a $170,000 base with no equity. Use our Job Offer Comparison Guide to evaluate full packages.
  4. Account for location. National medians are nearly useless for individual benchmarking. Always filter by metro area or compare remote-adjusted rates.
  5. Check recency. Tech compensation moved significantly from 2021–2024. Data older than 18 months may be materially outdated.

Industry Sector: A Bigger Factor Than Most Engineers Realize

Job Market Outlook: Is Software Engineering Still Worth Pursuing?

The software engineering job market in 2026 is more competitive than the 2020–2022 peak, but the long-term outlook remains strong. The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects 25% employment growth for software developers through 2032 — much faster than the 4% average for all occupations. That projection covers 411,400 new jobs over the decade.

The 2022–2024 tech layoffs reshaped the landscape but did not reverse the structural demand for software talent. What changed:

  • More selective hiring: Companies moved from "hire aggressively, manage out slowly" to "hire very selectively for immediate need"
  • AI integration creating new roles: AI engineering, prompt engineering, and LLM application development emerged as distinct specializations with strong demand
  • Non-tech demand surging: Financial services, healthcare, retail, and manufacturing are all accelerating digital transformation, driving SWE hiring outside traditional tech hubs
  • Remote work normalization: The talent market became more truly national, benefiting both engineers in lower-cost areas and companies in markets with limited local tech talent

The engineers most at risk are those in mid-level roles at overstaffed mature tech companies. Engineers with specialized AI/ML skills, strong systems expertise, or deep domain knowledge in high-value industries remain in high demand.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average software engineer salary in 2026?

The Bureau of Labor Statistics reports a median of $130,160 for software developers (May 2024 OEWS data). Glassdoor's March 2026 data shows a higher figure of $148,861 average base salary, reflecting its skew toward tech sector submissions. Both numbers are useful benchmarks, but neither tells the full story without accounting for company tier, location, and specialization.

Which state pays software engineers the most?

California pays the highest median at $170,910 (BLS 2024), but Washington offers the best after-tax value — comparable median wages with zero state income tax. For engineers optimizing take-home pay, Washington or Texas (no state income tax) are often more attractive than California despite California's nominally higher median.

How much does a junior software engineer make?

Entry-level software engineers (0–2 years) typically earn $75,000–$110,000 in base salary at mainstream employers. At large tech companies, new graduates earn $180,000–$230,000 in total compensation including equity. Bootcamp graduates generally start at $65,000–$95,000 per Glassdoor 2026 data.

What software engineering specialty pays the most?

Machine learning and AI engineering commands 20–40% above general SWE pay at equivalent seniority. Quantitative finance engineering (hedge funds, prop trading) has the highest ceiling — senior roles routinely exceed $500,000 total compensation. Security engineering earns a consistent 15–25% premium across all company types.

Is a $200K software engineer salary realistic in 2026?

Yes, for senior engineers at large tech companies or high-growth startups, particularly in high-cost metros. Glassdoor data shows the 75th percentile base salary at $189,039. Total compensation above $200K is achievable with 5+ years of experience at the right company tier. It is not a realistic target for most engineers at the BLS median employer level.

How do remote software engineer salaries compare?

Fully remote roles increasingly apply geographic pay adjustments. A senior role listed as remote might be offered at $280,000–$320,000 versus $380,000 for the same role based in Seattle — reflecting the company's cost-of-labor adjustment by the employee's home location. Some companies (notably Shopify, GitLab) pay globally competitive rates regardless of location; others (Google, Meta) apply significant reductions.

How fast do software engineer salaries grow with experience?

In the first 10 years, software engineer salaries grow faster than almost any other profession. Moving from junior to mid-level typically adds 30–50% to base salary. Senior to staff is a smaller base jump (10–20%) but often where equity grants become transformative. After 15 years, individual contributor growth slows unless you move into staff or principal roles.
